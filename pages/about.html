The goal of the Swarmathon competition project is to implement search algorithms on robotic swarms in order to collect resources as quickly and efficiently as possible with the goal of aiding future NASA space explorations.

This student driven project allows students to gain experience working collaboratively on a large complex software development project while learning how to research and develop search algorithms. In addition, it allows the students to gain experience in project management as well as enhance their communication and team skills. Student team members will also be going to local middle and high schools to share their experiences working on the swarm robotics team â€“ highlighting the engineering design challenges and solutions.

The competition is open to minority serving universities, and our diverse team of twenty students is a reflection of the ethnic diversity found on the Cal State LA campus. Our team consists of students majoring in electrical engineering, mechanical engineering, and computer science, showing diversity in under-represented members in these fields, including ethnic minorities and women.

Through our outreach efforts we hope to encourage more underrepresented minorities and girls to pursue careers in STEM.
